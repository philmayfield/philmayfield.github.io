Conni Product Maintenance

How do I wash and dry my Conni Bed Pad & underwear?

Simply put your Bed Pad or underwear into your washing machine and use a good quality laundry detergent. DO NOT use fabric softeners or chlorine bleach; if stains are present you may use oxygenating bleach. Do not wash or tumble dry hotter than 176ºF (80ºC). Your Bed Pad will also benefit from line drying in the sun if this option is available.

How often do I need to wash my Bed Pad?

If soiled during the night then you will need to wash each morning or approx every 8 hours if you are using the beds pad continually. If Bed Pad has not been soiled, then regularly wash with the rest of your bed linen will be fine.

Incontinence FAQ

How common is urinary incontinence?

Urinary incontinence, also known as bladder weakness is common, with approximately 25 million Americans experiencing this at some point in their lives. In the United States, approximately one in four women will experience bladder weakness, and women represent 75-80% of people with incontinence. Incontinence can also be experienced by children with either persistent or occasional bedwetting, which is distinct to the process of potty training.

Go back to top of page

What are the causes of urinary incontinence?

Incontinence frequently occurs in the elderly population, those who are ill and disabled, and can also occur following reproductive organ surgery such as prostate surgery. Women post-pregnancy may have weak pelvic floor muscles, and thickness and elasticity of the bladder and urethra can be affected by menopause resulting in bladder weakness. Woman are often affected by stress and/or urge incontinence (sometimes called Light Bladder Leakage or LBL) due to these two reasons. Other implicated factors can include dehydration, obesity urinary tract infection, smoking, diabetes.

How can I manage and prevent accidents?

There are a few things you can do to help manage and prevent accidents. Things you can do include regular Kegal pelvic floor exercises on a daily basis, make sure you drink 6-8 glasses of water per day, check and treat any urinary tract infection, reduce or eliminate caffeine and alcohol, maintain a healthy body weight, avoid smoking. Conni products can help manage accidents with an extensive range of absorbent and waterproof bed pads, waterproof bed, pillow and quilt protection, absorbent and waterproof sheet and pillow protection, absorbent and waterproof chair pads, absorbent and waterproof underwear for men, women and children, containment swim pants and absorbent floor mat.

What are Kegal exercises and what do they do?

Kegal exercises are a way of increasing the strength of your pelvic floor, which is important for supporting your bladder and other internal organs.

How can my child and I get a good night’s sleep?

Parents or carers can become frustrated with changing the bed during the night – it disrupts not only your sleep, but also the child’s, and there is the added concern of calming a child down after an accident. By using an absorbent and waterproof bed pad on top of the bottom sheet of the bed, along with a mattress protector (Toggles or Micro-Plush fitted sheet), it is as simple as taking the bed pad off the bed and replacing it with another. The process with a bed pad is much quicker and easier than a full bed change allowing you and the child to quickly return to bed, and potentially reducing the laundry the next day. If the child requires comforter/quilt protection, Conni can help with specific waterproof area protection.
